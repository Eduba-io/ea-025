01 / The Eduba frame

Most awardees automate the wrong things first.

About 60% of what a non-traditional defense awardee does every day is traditional code and database work. About 30% is rule-based logic. About 10% is a genuine AI problem. The job is sorting those three into the right layers before the clock starts.

Applied to a FUZE awardee, the map resolves quickly. Proposal libraries, test plans, after-action templates, and compliance evidence archives are database and code problems. DFARS, CMMC 2.0, ITAR gating, and ATO package routing are rule-based logic. The underlying technology (autonomy, EW, counter-UAS, energy resilience) is where the awardee's AI cycles should actually live.

A training layer that teaches new awardees to apply the 60 / 30 / 10 map to their own stack compresses their first ninety days and protects FUZE's 60 to 70 day contracting goal.
